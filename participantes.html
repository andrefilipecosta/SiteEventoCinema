Participantes

Aldo Bearzatto

Passionate about cities and cinema, is the co-founder and artistic director of Fstvl Close-Up (Paris and Île-de-France) and Ecrans Urbains (Lausanne). He also produces and hosts the radio show La Fabrique Urbaine.

Christian Barani

Is building a practice that combines documentary and visual arts. The filmmaking process involves an experience involving a body/camera walking through space. To film, he defines a set of rules that incorporate chance and improvisation. There is no pre-script; walking is used as an invitation to encounter. Everything is played out in the moment. At the end of the experience, material is collected; it will take different forms depending on the upcoming project and the exhibition venue: film, installation, performance, etc.

Hervé Bougon

Passionate about cinema since his early childhood, is the co-founder and artistic director of Fstvl Close-Up, a programmer for the War on Screen Festival, and a film consultant for the private sector.

Jean-Yves Leloup

Is Considered one of the pioneers of "cinemix" and the new wave of film concerts that emerged in the mid-2000s in France, he uses pre-existing music with timeless overtones for his soundtracks, composed by a wide range of artists, from contemporary music to electronic music, music for the image to pop. Using editing, collage and remix techniques, he gives a new dimension to the film in order to give it emotion as well as a more contemporary dimension. He is the author of five essential books on electronic music like Electro: from Kraftwerk to Daft Punk (2019).
